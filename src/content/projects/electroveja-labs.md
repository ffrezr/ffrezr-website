## "We are what androids dream of."

Electroveja Labs was born inside the halls of Universidad Técnica Federico Santa María as a collective of engineers, artists, and scientists. The name is a nod to Philip K. Dick's *Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?* — we wanted every visitor to step into dreamlike experiences where science meets art.

### What we built

We specialized in **interactive digital experiences** that combine artificial intelligence, computer vision, projection mapping, sensors, and augmented reality. Our three service lines were:

- **AI-powered installations** — Deep Learning and Computer Vision models deployed in real-world museum and exhibition settings.
- **Space digitization** — Transforming physical exhibition spaces into interactive environments with gesture-based interaction.
- **Interactive visual experiences** — Projection mapping, sensor-driven installations, and video games bridging the physical and virtual worlds.

### Selected projects

**Van Gogh te pinta** (2020) — Museo Artequín Viña del Mar & UTFSM
An interactive, multisensory installation placed next to a real representation of Van Gogh's *Bedroom in Arles*. Using AI, visitors could see themselves rendered in Van Gogh's distinctive brushstroke style through short looping videos. It became a hit with younger visitors who shared their generated portraits on social media.

**PintaPeces** (2021) — AC3E & Instituto de Fomento Pesquero (IFOP)
An interactive digital aquarium created for Chile's Festival de la Ciencia (FECI). Users downloaded fish templates, colored them, photographed them, and uploaded them to a website — their drawings came to life in a virtual fishbowl live-streamed on YouTube. The project generated so much engagement that IFOP adopted it as a platform for marine biodiversity education in the Valparaíso region.

**Estrellas en el Cañón** (2019) — UTFSM & Astronomy Club
A projection mapping intervention on the historic cannon at UTFSM's emblematic *Patio del Cañón*. We used a laser projector — chosen specifically to minimize light pollution — to display stars and native flowers during stargazing events organized by the university's Astronomy Club.

**Muralla Interactiva** (2019) — UTFSM
An interactive wall where visitors became part of the composition through light and shadow projections, complete with trail effects and 80s-style flash visuals. Installed in the university's assembly hall.

**Alimentando Peces** (2019) — UTFSM
Our very first proof of concept: a virtual environment where children played surrounded by digital fish. Used during guided school visits to the university.

### My role

As CEO and Co-Founder, I led the technical and strategic vision — from client discovery and requirement gathering to training, validating, and deploying the Deep Learning and Computer Vision models that powered our installations. I worked alongside a multidisciplinary team that included a video game designer, an electronics MSc engineer, and a UTFSM associate professor as advisor.
